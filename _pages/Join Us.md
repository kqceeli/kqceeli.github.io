# Join Us

I am joining the **School of Water Resources and Hydropower Engineering, Wuhan University**, where I am building a new research group at the frontier of **geomechanics, computational mechanics, uncertainty quantification, and artificial intelligence**.

Our ambition is to develop an **internationally leading research group** that advances both fundamental science and high-impact engineering solutions for the next generation of geotechnical, hydraulic, energy, and infrastructure systems.

<div class="join-tagline">
We are looking for curious, rigorous, and ambitious people who want to work on problems that no single discipline can solve alone.
</div>

</div>

---

## Why This Group?

Modern geotechnical and hydraulic engineering is entering a new era.  
Deep underground construction, climate-driven permafrost degradation, offshore energy infrastructure, aging dams and tunnels, and uncertain geo-hazards all demand methods that go beyond traditional engineering practice.

Our group is built around a central idea:

> **The future of geomechanics lies in the integration of physics, computation, data, and intelligence.**

We aim to create models, algorithms, and engineering tools that are not only mathematically rigorous and computationally powerful, but also physically interpretable, experimentally grounded, and useful for real-world decision-making.

---

<div class="section-title">Research Philosophy</div>

<div class="card-grid">

<div class="info-card">

### Physics First, Data Smart

We develop models that respect the laws of mechanics while learning from experimental, field, and simulation data. The goal is reliability beyond a training dataset.

</div>

<div class="info-card">

### Uncertainty as Information

Geomaterials are heterogeneous, data are sparse, and environments are changing. We quantify uncertainty rigorously and turn it into safer engineering decisions.

</div>

<div class="info-card">

### AI for Scientific Discovery

We use machine learning not as a black box, but as a tool to discover constitutive laws, multiscale patterns, hidden variables, and failure mechanisms.

</div>

<div class="info-card">

### Theory–Simulation–Experiment Loop

We believe real progress comes from closing the loop between mathematical theory, numerical simulation, laboratory experiments, and field observations.

</div>

</div>
